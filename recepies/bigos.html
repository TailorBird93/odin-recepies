Description

Bigos is a traditional Polish dish. It can be described as a one-pot hunter's stew.

There are as many variations of bigos as there are people cooking it, a common quality of all stews.

It does, however, possess a base list of ingredients that simply must be in it:

  • White cabbage
  • Sour cabbage aka Sauerkraut
  • Smoked kie≈Çbasa sausage

Those make up the backbone of any decent bigos.

Let's get to the full list of ingredients needed for my version of bigos

Ingredients:

  • White cabbage (1 Head)
  • Sour cabbage (500g)
  • Smoked sausage (pack of 6)
  • Pork shoulder fillets (400g)
  • Brown onion (1 cannonball, or few smaller ones)
  • Prunes (40g)
  • Dried mushrooms (40g)
  • Chopped tomatoes (3 cans)
  • Splash of red wine
  • Spices:
    • Garlic powder
    • Smoked paprika
    • Salt and pepper
    • Pack of bigos spice mix (optional)

Steps:

  1. Dice the onion
  2. Cut the sausage into slices
  3. Cut the pork shoulder fillets into bite-size chunks
  4. Put all 3 into a warmed up pot and start to fry them
  5. Add red wine and reduce it
  6. After the onion gets golden brown chop up the white cabbage and place in the pot
  7. Wait for it to wilt a bit
  8. Add the sour cabbage and spices
  9. After about 10 minutes, add the prunes and dried mushrooms
  10. While stirring, add chopped tomatoes
  11. Leave to boil on low heat for about 2 hours
  12. Serve and enjoy!
